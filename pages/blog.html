  • PSRs and OHRC

    The Moon’s permanently shadowed regions (PSRs) are enigmatic zones bathed in perpetual darkness, making their imaging a complex endeavor. The OHRC (Orbiter High-Resolution Camera) on Chandrayaan-2 is uniquely equipped to tackle this challenge, capturing the faint and elusive light that trickles from these shadowed areas. Through the OHRC’s high-resolution capabilities, we gain access to unparalleled detail in these mysterious regions, transforming the obscured darkness into a window of discovery, revealing the Moon's most secretive landscapes.

  • HYPERSPECTRAL IMAGING TECHNIQUES

    Hyperspectral imaging techniques unravel the intricate tapestry of light by capturing a vast spectrum across multiple wavelengths. Through advanced methods like spectral unmixing and principal component analysis, these techniques decompose complex scenes into their purest spectral signatures, revealing hidden materials and subtle features. With tools such as band ratioing and wavelet transforms, hyperspectral imaging enhances detail and contrast, turning intricate light data into a rich, high-definition portrait of the scene, ideal for precise material identification and comprehensive analysis.
